## 1. Document Purpose

This Brand Brief documents the core strategic identity of **DevSpirits** (the product) and **IdanLevianDeveloper** (the brand). It defines how the brand communicates, who it serves, why it exists, and how it should be represented across the DevSpirits landing page, messaging, design, and collaboration. A Brand Brief ensures clarity and consistency in all strategic and creative outputs.

---

## 2. DevSpirits — Product Overview

**Product Name:** DevSpirits  
**Purpose:** A landing/SEO/geo page designed to generate leads for freelance developer projects.

**Primary Goal:**  
Convert visitors into leads — contact form, CTA, and bookings.

**Secondary Goals:**  
- SEO visibility and discoverability  
- Geo-targeted content (city/region pages)  
- Trust signals: portfolio, testimonials, skills, certifications  

**Content Structure (Landing Page):**  
- **Hero:** Headline + subheadline + primary CTA above the fold  
- **Value Proposition:** What you offer, why it matters  
- **Social Proof:** Testimonials, logos, case studies  
- **Call-to-Action:** Repeated at logical points  
- **Contact:** Form or clear contact method  

**Conversion Principles:**  
- Clear CTAs (e.g. "Get a Quote", "Contact", "Book a Call")  
- Minimal friction: short forms, clear value proposition  
- Mobile-first: forms and CTAs must work well on small screens

## 7. Ideal Audience

**Primary Audience:**  
- Small business owners with a growth mindset.  
- Entrepreneurs with strong product vision.  
- Clients who value long-term quality and thoughtful technology.

**Non-Ideal Audience:**  
- Clients focused solely on lowest cost.  
- Those who undervalue process, discipline, or professional standards.

A strong brand brief ensures your audience is clearly defined so communication is intentional — and DevSpirits should speak directly to the primary audience.
